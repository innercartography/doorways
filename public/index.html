01 · The claim

Cities already know their own future.

A property manager knows which retail spaces will go dark. The barista knows the block is turning over. The permit clerk knows what's coming before the crane arrives.

This knowledge is real, distributed, and unpriced. There is no instrument for it — only rumor.

03 · The domain edge

Not all rent is uncertain.

San Francisco has three regulatory tiers. Knowing which is which is the difference between a live market and a dead one:

Rent Ordinance / pre-1979

Increase is a published CPI formula. In a hot market landlords always max it. No disagreement — no market.

AB 1482 / state cap

Capped at 5% + CPI. Also below market, also maxed. Deterministic — no market.

New builds / uncapped

The only tier where the increase is a real decision. Here — and only here — asking-rent markets have genuine uncertainty.

So the uncertainty doesn't live in "will rent rise." It lives upstream, in the CPI input that sets every capped increase — and downstream, in the uncapped tier. We build markets on those two, not the dead middle.

04 · The oracle recipe

Every market declares how it settles.

Trust isn't assumed — it's specified, like an ingredient list. Three data tiers, three honesty levels, each named before you trade:

API-resolved · live on stage

Permits settle from DataSF p4e4-a5a7; CPI markets from the BLS release. One field, zero judgment. This is the market we resolve in front of you.

Public-data-computed

Rent buckets from the Rent Board inventory (gdc7-dmcn) — authoritative, penalty-of-perjury, but annual and coarse. Method published up front.

Attestation

Storefront closures via staked local observers. Subjective, small-scale — the frontier problem, named honestly, not faked.

The tier is the honesty. Trustworthy-but-slow, or fast-but-proprietary — we price the tradeoff instead of hiding it.

05 · The innovation

Insiders attest. Outsiders trade.

Some knowledge is expertise. Some is influence over the outcome. A market has to tell them apart — and a property operator feels the line from the inside:

Fair — expertise

Knowing the CPI formula sets capped increases. Public rules, privately understood. This edge belongs in trading.

Foul — influence

Knowing you're about to re-list a specific unit at market. Private action you control. This belongs in attestation, never trading.

Participants who can move a resolution are flagged: barred from trading it, routed instead to attest. Being an insider becomes a credential, not a weapon.

08 · For agents, specifically

This page is also an API.

Everything above is the same information a human gets by reading. An agent gets it by calling — no scraping, no guessing at DOM structure. That's the point of building this in WebMCP-native form from day one, not bolting it on later.

Why no commerce protocols (x402, MPP, UCP, ACP)?
Deliberately, not by omission. Every market here is play money — accuracy is scored in reputation, not dollars, precisely because the real-money regulatory picture (Kalshi/CFTC) for event contracts is still unsettled. Wiring up an agentic-commerce payment rail on top of an instrument we're not licensed to settle in real money would be the wrong kind of "agent-ready." We'd rather score zero here honestly than fake a rail with nothing legal underneath it.
Why does the permit market sometimes resolve to "not yet"?
Because that's the honest live answer. 915 Bryant St is still in triage as of this build — the resolver reads DataSF's real record, not a script written to make the demo land on a satisfying YES. If it resolves live in front of you and comes back resolved: false, that's the mechanism working, not failing.
What happens if DataSF or BLS is unreachable?
Both resolvers fail over to a pre-fetched snapshot in /data and label the evidence accordingly (source will say "cached" instead of the live URL). Same resolution logic either way — tested by killing the network mid-call, not assumed.
Is trading here durable?
Yes — every trade is written as its own immutable record to a shared log (Vercel Blob), and the price you see is replayed from that full history, not held in one function's memory. Two different people curling this at the same moment both land in the same market, the same way two Postgres clients see the same table. One caveat, named honestly: writes aren't wrapped in a database transaction, so two trades landing in the same instant could theoretically interleave — acceptable for a demo, not how we'd ship a real-money version.
Why no A2A Agent Card, if there's already an MCP server?
Same discipline as the oracle recipes: don't publish a capability without the substance behind it. An A2A Agent Card advertises a real agent-to-agent task-delegation endpoint (JSON-RPC, task lifecycle, the works) — we haven't built that, only tool-calling via MCP, so publishing the card would be a claim with nothing behind it. Named honestly here rather than faked, same as the attestation tier.
Who is "seed-bot" in the activity feed?
There's no cron on this deploy, so a market sitting at an untouched, never-traded 50/50 would look dead the moment this page goes live. Any market with zero trades gets exactly one small trade from a userId literally named seed-bot — bounded, one-shot per market, never disguised as a person. Everything after that first trade is real: you, or whoever scans the QR next.

09 · The horizon

Markets at the scale of a block.

A neighborhood is a tribe with concentrated knowledge. Give it an instrument and its wisdom becomes legible — to itself first, and to capital on the tribe's terms.

Later: autonomous resolvers with onchain identity, staked reputation, and provenance for every settlement. Instruments, not oracles-by-decree.

Places hold knowledge. This makes it tradable without making it extractable.
